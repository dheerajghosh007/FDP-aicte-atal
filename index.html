About

The relational database (RDBMS) model completely dominated database technology for over 20 years. Today this “one size fits all” stability has been disrupted by a relatively recent explosion of new database technologies. These paradigm-busting technologies are powering the “Big Data” and “NoSQL” revolutions, as well as forcing fundamental changes in databases across the board. Today we are faced with choices between radically different database technologies. To handle semi-structured and un-structured data, the traditional databases have been proven incapable, so Next Generation databases are required to address these issues. Next Generation Databases demystifies today’s new database technologies. In this FDP we will be focussing on understanding the Next Generation Databases and know practically how they can be used to manage semi-structured and unstructured data.

Topics

Topics to be Covered:

  • Overview of NoSQL databases.
  • Members of NoSQL family.
  • Issues with distributed databases.
  • CAP theorem.
  • Learning Bias and Variance
  • System design issues with NoSQL databases.
  • Document Databases – Case study of MongoDB
  • CRUD operations in MongoDB.
  • Using MongoDB on cloud.
  • Sharding, Replication, performance tuning and other advanced operations in MongoDB.
  • Graph Databases - Case study of Neo4j.
  • CRUD operations in Neo4j.
  • Key-Valued Databases – Case study of Redis.
  • Key-Valued Databases – Case study of Amazon DynamoDB.

About

The Host Institute (BIT, Durg)

Bhilai Institute of Technology, Durg affiliated to Chhattisgarh Swami Vivekanand Technical University Bhilai initiated by notable stalwarts of central India in 1986 with a vision to become one of the Premier Technical and Management Educational Institutions in the Country, delivering excellent professional education, creating value based, globally competitive professionals and achieving excellence in all our endeavors BIT campus has a conducive and supportive academic ambience facilitating high quality education and research for more than 4000 students of bachelors, masters and doctoral programs. BIT, Durg is the first technical institute in the state to be conferred Autonomous Status by UGC, Government of India. Seven of its undergraduate programs have been accredited by NBA and the institute itself has been “A” grade accreditation by NAAC. The institute stands in NIRF 2020 Rank-band: 251-300 in the category of Engineering. Under AICTE Margdarshan Scheme, the institute is mentoring ten technical institutions to encourage best practices.
